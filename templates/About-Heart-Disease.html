Heart Disease

What is Heart Disease ?

Heart disease prediction refers to the use of various techniques and tools to estimate an individual's risk of developing cardiovascular disease (CVD) or other related conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. These techniques may involve the analysis of various risk factors such as age, gender, family history, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and lifestyle habits. The aim of heart disease prediction is to identify individuals who may be at a higher risk of developing heart disease so that they can be targeted with preventive measures such as lifestyle modifications, medication, or other interventions that can reduce the risk of developing CVD. By predicting and managing the risk of heart disease, healthcare professionals can help individuals maintain a healthy heart and reduce the risk of developing serious cardiovascular conditions. Heart disease, also known as cardiovascular disease, refers to a group of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. The most common types of heart disease include coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and heart valve problems.

What are the symptoms of Heart Disease ?

1. Chest pain or discomfort

2. Shortness of breath

3. Fatigue

4. Swelling in the legs, ankles, or feet

5. Irregular heartbeat

6. Dizziness or lightheadedness

7. Nausea or vomiting

Precautions that can help prevent heart disease include:

1. Quit smoking

2. Exercise regularly

3. Manage your blood pressure

4. Manage your cholesterol

5. Manage your blood sugar

6. Manage your weight

7. Limit alcohol
