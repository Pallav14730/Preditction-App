About Heart Disease Prediction

Heart disease prediction refers to the use of various techniques and tools to estimate an individual's risk of developing cardiovascular disease (CVD) or other related conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. These techniques may involve the analysis of various risk factors such as age, gender, family history, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and lifestyle habits. The aim of heart disease prediction is to identify individuals who may be at a higher risk of developing heart disease so that they can be targeted with preventive measures such as lifestyle modifications, medication, or other interventions that can reduce the risk of developing CVD. By predicting and managing the risk of heart disease, healthcare professionals can help individuals maintain a healthy heart and reduce the risk of developing serious cardiovascular conditions.
